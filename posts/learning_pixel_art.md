# Learning Pixel Art
##### 2020.09.06

## Why start?
My main intent with starting pixel art, was to have an easy way to get decent looking assets for my games. I didn't want to keep the art in my games as squares, but I also didn't want to worry about finding the right assets on something like [open game art](https://opengameart.org/), or finding someone willing to do art for me.

## First Steps
It is harder to find a program that can't be used for pixel art in than it is to find one that can. Excel? Change the cells to squares and fill them one by one. DAWs? Make a bunch of differently colored tracks and zoom properly. Notepad? Write the correct characters, then change the txt file to a .bmp and you have an image. If you want an actual art program, there are millions of art programs, apps and web pages for free. Point is, you can start with anything. The program I first chose to use was the free trial of [paint tool sai](https://www.systemax.jp/en/sai/) (which could be indefinitely renewed by deleting the folder in ProgramData). For a few weeks I tried drawing one thing every day. I saw fast improvement as I started to understand how to represent detail at such a low resolution. Sadly, all my first pieces were lost when my robotics programming team had to perform emergency surgery on my laptop.

## Motivation
Since art is way down on the list of things I enjoy doing, getting the motivation to learn more was difficult. Pixel art is one of those things that I enjoy the result of, just not the process. To get around this problem I force my friends to learn with me. Me and 2 others set up a pixel-art channel in our discord and started all drawing off of a prompt and posting the results. It made it much more enjoyable now that it was more of a competition to see who could draw the best thing. Also, the added pressure of people waiting for you to finish made me draw more efficiently.

## Art Programs
I soon grew tired of paint tool sai and wanted more features, so sometime late 2019, I started making my [own pixel art program](https://rsninja.dev/notGames/pixelJS/index.html). I worked on it on and off for 6 months making it my biggest coding project so far in terms of complexity. Even in the incomplete state it is currently, it had way more features for pixel art than paint tool sai. I used it for many months, even using the program to make all the images used in the UI of the program. I learned a lot in making it, but the biggest thing I learned was that [aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org/) had all the features I wanted. Before starting to make my program I had taken a quick look at aseprite and thought it was missing things I wanted for some reason. I still don't regret spending 6 months making my own.

## What I Have Learned
after 3 years of working on my pixel art skills here is what I have learned:

#### resolution
When choosing what size to draw at, always start with the face, or smallest detail that needs to be shown, and adjust the canvas size later.

#### references
using references when drawing is essential to getting proportions and colors correct.

#### detail
Having large splotches of pixels that are all the same color is distracting, and generally looks bad. Almost everything in real life has some kind of texture or lighting, so solid color doesn't look normal to us.

#### color
My color choice isn't as good as I'd like, but I understand the basic concepts of shadows being colder colors and highlights being warmer colors.

#### backgrounds
having foreground and background gives a piece context and generally makes it look better.

#### other stuff
Because I am still learning, there are many concepts that I sort of understand and unconsciously use when doing pixel art. I just don't have a strong enough understanding to be able to put it into words.
