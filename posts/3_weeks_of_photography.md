# 3 Weeks of photography
##### 2020.08.23

## Camera

After 5 months of quarantine, I was bored enough to indulge in the best past time of any good capitalist society; Buying things I don't really need.
A couple days of research led to settling on the [Nikon D3500](https://www.nikonusa.com/en/nikon-products/product/dslr-cameras/d3500.html). It has specs that seemed good enough, and it is small enough to carry around without to much of a hassle.
My biggest priority was making sure the camera could take better pictures than the one on my $100 phone. I also made sure to get a DSLR because that seemed like the cool thing to do.

![](media/photography1.jpg)

## Getting started 

All my previous photography experience was taking the occasional picture with my phone, or ipod. As soon as the camera arrived (it only took a week which was impressive for quarantine shipping times) I read through the whole manual like an absolute nerd. Then I Watched a few videos on basics of photography. Most of them layed out a bunch of rules that seemed like good advice, but at the end of the video they would say the rules are ment to be broken, so I stopped watching those quickly. Seriously tutorial man, should I, or should I not use the rule of thirds? I  need answers.

My first shooting session (is that the correct term?) consisted of taking the same picture of a tree 30 times, changing settings every time. I hardly learned anything about what number means what, so I continued on and just tried to take pictures of what I thought looked good. I think I was shooting manually for half the time I was there.

![](media/photography2.jpg)

30 attempts for this? what a newb.

## Learning

Early on I went almost fully manual. There are just a few things like white balance that I leave on auto that I might look into later. Mostly my time has been spent getting used to ISO, aperture, and shutter speed. I feel fairly knowledgeable about each, but It still takes me a few tries to get the result I want for each picture. I haven't spent much more time following tutorials, though I probably should. I haven't really planned put how I'm going to learn. I just make sure to shoot sort of often and try to enjoy the process.

## Average Shoot 

For now, I mostly take images of static things. I keep the ISO between 200 and 1600. Aperture stay around 4 if I want one thing in focus, or 20ish if I want most of the picture focused. I rely mostly on shutter speed to get the right amount of light. Using a long exposure usually isn't a problem since I carry a old tripod. It is only ~1m high, but it does the job.

I'll capture around 100 photos in a shoot and delete around 80%. out of the remaining photos I only edit and post around 3. 

I always set out with a picture in mind. I sometimes can get what I envisioned, but most of the time I will get lucky with some mundane place that turns out looking good. The best example of this is a concrete sitting area in a downtown plaza. I had a few LEDs which made it look kind of interesting as long as I got down close enough to the ground. I was very close to just walking past without batting an eye.

![](media/photography3.jpg)

## Editing

I like to keep my photos fairly close to what was originally captured. Now I tend to stick to cropping, adjusting levels and curves, then maybe sharpening the image. I use GNU's Not Unix Not Unix Image Manipulation Program for my editing I made fun of the program for a while, until I got it and saw it defaulted to dark theme.

## Future

I look forwards to learning more about my camera, and photography in general. I want to go to new locations and photograph some more impressive areas. Concrete benches are cool and all, but I want to see some cooler stuff.

## Where I Post

Currently I post on this site as well as Instagram. I don't follow or respond to anything on Instagram because I like using it as a sort of gallery. I also don't need more social media in my life. Eventually I want a place to put all the photos I keep so they aren't just sitting on my SSD.
